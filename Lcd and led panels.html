LCD & LED Panels

By 2004 Samsung was the world's-largest manufacturer of OLEDs, with a 40 percent market share worldwide,[56] and as of 2010 has a 98% share of the global AMOLED market.[57] The company generated $100.2 million out of the total $475 million revenues in the global OLED market in 2006.[58] As of 2006, it held more than 600 American patents and more than 2,800 international patents, making it the largest owner of AMOLED technology patents.[58] Samsung's current AMOLED smartphones use its Super AMOLED trademark, with the Samsung Wave S8500 and Samsung i9000 Galaxy S being launched in June 2010. In January 2011, it announced its Super AMOLED Plus displays[59] – which offer several advances over the older Super AMOLED displays – real stripe matrix (50 percent more sub pixels), thinner form factor, brighter image and an 18 percent reduction in energy consumption.
